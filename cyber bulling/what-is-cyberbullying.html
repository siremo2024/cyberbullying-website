What is Cyberbullying?

Definition

Cyberbullying involves the use of technology such as social media, email, or messaging to harass, threaten, or embarrass another person. It is typically a repeated, aggressive behavior aimed at causing harm or distress.

Where It Happens

Cyberbullying often takes place on platforms that students use daily:

  • Social media (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok)
  • Messaging apps (WhatsApp, iMessage)
  • Public forums (Reddit, Discord)

Types of Cyberbullying

  • Harassment: Sending offensive or threatening messages.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone else to cause harm.
  • Exclusion: Deliberately excluding someone from online groups or activities.
  • Outing: Sharing private information or images without consent.

    • Why It's Serious

    Cyberbullying can have severe consequences because it spreads quickly, reaches a large audience, and often remains online permanently.
